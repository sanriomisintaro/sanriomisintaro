Basic Info About Me

Sanriomi Sintaro, S.Kom., M.Kom is a Lecturer in the Information System, Sam Ratulangi University, Indonesia. Prior to joining Sam Ratulangi University, he obtained his Master from the faculty of Computer Science, Budi Luhur University, Indonesia.


Sanriomi is interested in the following research fields:
• Cultural Heritage Informatics
• Educational Technology
• Game-based Learning
• Information System


In particular, Sanriomi aims to apply games and technology in the development of education and also the preservation of cultural heritage. Sanriomi believes that education for the future and information on cultural heritage in the past can only be achieved in the present.


Right now, I am focused to develop game-based learning or cultural heritage informatics with a website as the platform. You can look at my portfolio to know more about my project. For prospective students: I am always looking for self-motivated students with a strong interest in research on game-based education or cultural heritage informatics. I’m also happy when there are students who want to discuss more about research or competition that you want to achieve. If you are interested, don't hesitate to get contact me via sanriomi@unsrat.ac.id.

    hosted by Research and Development Center for Lampung Province. I made a lightweight and cost-effective 2in1 fruit dryer that can be used when there is enough heat from the sun and can be folded if you want to use electrical energy from the heat of an incandescent lamp.

  • 2020

    Grand finalist for the kihajar mobile application competition

    Culture and education multimedia development center

    I made mobile game with HTML5 game engine, Percobaan Virtual Listrik (Electricity Virtual Experiment). A simple application for carrying out simple experiments that educators can use as a tool when teaching by paying attention to existing material and accompanied by appropriate real-life applications. With this application, it is hoped that it can help educators to be able to educate students in a unique and interesting way. This contest is hosted by Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology.

  • 2019

    Grand finalist for the kihajar mobile application competition

    Culture and education multimedia development center

    I made mobile game with HTML5 game engine, Tebak Tanda !? (Guess the Sign!?). This is a simple game about fighting Indonesian skills to guess exclamation points and question marks in a sentence. Even though it looks simple, there is an educational ~Fun Battle Game~ element in it. This contest is hosted by Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology.

  • 2018

    Grand finalist for the kihajar mobile application competition

    Culture and education multimedia development center

    I made mobile game with HTML5 game engine, Aljabar Puzzle. This is a math game focused on simple Algebra. Packed with unique and challenging gravity puzzles.I thought of an idea about a simple educational game that can be a medium of learning both in class and at home. So, in Algebra Puzzle, there is a menu that can be used by teachers or student guardians to be able to create questions dynamically, so that students can work on questions that are not monotonous and of course more exciting and fun. This contest is hosted by Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology.
